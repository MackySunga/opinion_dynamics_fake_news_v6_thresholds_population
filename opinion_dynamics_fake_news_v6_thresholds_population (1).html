Fake News Dynamics with Population and Threshold Controls

A false claim does not simply appear, get shared once, and disappear. It enters a network, interacts with people, gets reinforced by communities, amplified by coordinated actors, pushed by algorithms, resisted by correction, and then evolves over time.

These change how polarized and amplification-prone the network is.

2. Pick a mitigation strategy

Minimal mitigation is fast but weak. Strong counter-campaigns are slower but stronger and broader.

3. Population and thresholds

Ordinary users become believers once their belief exceeds the believer threshold, and become peddlers once it exceeds the higher peddler threshold. Special accounts such as persistent sources, troll farms, and bots remain role-based actors.

Simple Model Equation

A simplified opinion update rule with platform and mitigation effects can be written as:

bᵢ(t+1) = clip[ bᵢ(t) + α( b̄ᴺᵢ(t) - bᵢ(t) ) + δ( b̄ᴳᵢ(t) - bᵢ(t) ) + βSᵢ(t) + ηQᵢ(t) + κTᵢ(t) + ρBᵢ(t) + ωAᵢ(t) - ψMᵢ(t-τ)/(1 + λ·max(bᵢ(t),0) + φFᵢ(t)) , -1, 1 ]
bᵢ(t) = current belief of person i, from -1 (skeptical) to +1 (believes misinformation).
Thresholds: if $$b_i(t) > \theta_b$$, an ordinary user becomes a believer; if $$b_i(t) > \theta_p$$, the user becomes a peddler. In the simulation, you control these thresholds directly with the dials above.
α = neighbor influence. δ = echo-chamber reinforcement. β, η, κ, ρ, ω = misinformation channels. ψ = mitigation strength. τ = mitigation delay. λ = reversal difficulty. φ = fatigue effect.

Why the new controls matter

The new population and threshold controls make the state transitions more explicit. You can now directly test how many users become believers or peddlers when the network is larger, when the believer threshold is lower, or when the peddler threshold is set closer to the believer threshold.

  • Larger population: more nodes, more pathways for spread, and potentially more persistent misinformation waves.
  • Lower believer threshold: users become believers more easily.
  • Lower peddler threshold: believers start actively spreading misinformation earlier.
  • Higher thresholds: misinformation must build more pressure before users convert.
Say it simply: “This version lets us control how easily people become fake-news believers or peddlers, and how population size changes the difficulty of mitigation.”
